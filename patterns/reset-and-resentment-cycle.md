# Pattern: Reset and Resentment Cycle

**First observed:** 2026-04-19 (session 4)
**Confirmed:** 2026-04-19 follow-up call (session 5)
**Significant evolution:** 2026-05-02 (session 8b)
**Third state observed:** 2026-05-03 (session 9)

## Description

Specific to the relationship with Cyrus. Originally described as: Yinnie identifies a problem, allows normal life to resume before genuine change is demonstrated, then resents him at the next trigger. She identified this mechanism herself in session 4.

## Evolution

**Phase 1 - Reset and resent (sessions 4-6):** She kept accepting the return to normal before change was demonstrated. Tension dissolves, warmth resumes, she goes with it. Resentment builds for the next trigger.

**Phase 2 - Vigilance and exhaust (session 8):** She stopped prematurely resetting and started watching carefully. But the vigilance itself became a problem - she acknowledged being on "high alert" and potentially discounting genuine change. Exhausting and distorting.

**Phase 3 - Direct request and honest assessment (session 9):** She made a comprehensive, direct request. Cyrus initially pushed back, then delivered. She arrived at two formulations: "He's someone who needs to be asked" (functional assessment of the dynamic) and "I don't like it but I can live with it" (honest middle position on the cost). This is the first time the cycle was interrupted by direct action producing a visible outcome, leading to neither catastrophe nor premature resolution.

## Current State

The phase 3 position is new and untested under pressure. The holiday itself (5 days in Montenegro with his friends) will be the real test. The formulations from session 9 are the anchors:
- "He's someone who needs to be asked" - reframes the question from "is he broken?" to "is this cost acceptable?"
- "I don't like it but I can live with it" - honest middle, tolerating discomfort without either resetting or catastrophising.

The self-questioning capacity ("am I projecting? am I hyper alert?") observed in sessions 8-9 is part of what makes phase 3 possible. She is no longer just watching him - she is also watching herself.

## Clinical Notes

The structural concern about people-pleasing remains legitimate. Cyrus has said he doesn't understand why people-pleasing is a bad thing. If this is accurate, he is unlikely to take initiative on protecting her needs against his social circle. The functional question is whether she can sustain the cost of always asking directly, and whether his responsiveness to direct requests is consistent enough to build on.

The October wound is still present. "I don't like it" carries the weight of the original injury - being with someone who does not instinctively protect her. Whether the dynamic she described in session 9 can hold that weight long-term is the open question.

The mirrored people-pleasing observation (session 8) was not active in session 9 - she was direct rather than accommodating. One data point.

## Watch For

- Whether "I don't like it but I can live with it" holds on the actual holiday.
- Whether she makes direct requests in real time during the trip or reverts to pre-calculating.
- Whether Cyrus's responsiveness to direct requests is consistent or deteriorates when his friends are physically present.
- Whether a new incident destabilises the "he's someone who needs to be asked" formulation.
- Whether she raises the people-pleasing concern with Cyrus directly (he doesn't see it as a problem) or only discusses it in coaching.
- Whether the self-questioning capacity ("am I projecting?") remains accessible under stress.
- The parallel between her pre-calculation/accommodation and his people-pleasing - if it recurs, name it.
