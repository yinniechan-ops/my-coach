# Pattern: Net Negative Framing

**First observed:** 2026-04-16 (session 2)

## Description

Yinnie holds a belief that if her entrepreneurial effort does not succeed, she will be "net negative" - worse off than peers who took conventional paths. This is described as longstanding and has sharpened over three years of sustained effort.

## Evolution Across Sessions

- **Session 2:** First stated. "Unless I succeed in being an entrepreneur, I just end up being that person who didn't have a life."
- **Session 6:** Strongest activation. "Two years, absolutely no results." Resisted reframe: "I don't want you to glaze this over."
- **Session 7:** Potential inflection point. She made a grounded assessment of Daily (no PMF) that was separate from the global narrative.
- **Session 8:** Widened and deepened. Now multi-person comparisons (Mimi, friend at YC startup). She resisted the coach's attempt to separate the comparison more forcefully than before. The deepest version yet: "I don't bring anything other than my pure grit" - which is not just "I'm behind" but "I have nothing to offer."

## Current State

The frame is operating at two levels:
1. **Comparative:** She is watching peers advance (higher salaries, funded startups, podcast launches) while she is at 85k with a shelved product. She resists any suggestion that these are different situations.
2. **Identity-level:** "I don't bring anything" goes beyond comparison to a self-assessment of fundamental inadequacy. This is new as of session 8 and is the deepest activation.

The fragile separation observed in session 7 (grounded assessment vs. global narrative) was not visible in session 8a. The peer comparison spiral pulled her back into the global frame. However, the session 7 capacity may still be accessible - it was present once and may return in less depleted states.

## Clinical Notes

The frame is factually miscalibrated. Yinnie has relocated internationally twice, left a toxic role, shipped two working applications while employed full-time, and sustained three years of independent effort after a cofounder who didn't care walked away.

Simple counter-evidence no longer suffices. She actively resists reframes she experiences as positive glossing. The intervention that has the most traction is specific-evidence challenge ("you shipped two apps while employed full-time, you sustained three years after a cofounder walked away") rather than general reassurance.

Behavioural evidence (a cofounder who wants to work with her, a coffee that goes well, one person who uses something she builds) is more likely to move this belief than reframing.

The widening of peer comparisons (from one person to multiple) suggests the frame is generalising. This is consistent with the chronic low mood as a maintaining factor.

## Watch For

- Multi-person comparisons as the frame generalises.
- "I don't bring anything" or variants as the deepest activation.
- Resistance to separation of comparisons - she will push back on "that's not the same race."
- Whether the session 7 capacity (grounded assessment separate from global narrative) returns.
- Whether behavioural evidence (Rachel coffee, partnership prospect) can move the frame when reframing cannot.
- Pre-calculation of failure as a variant: "what's the point" applied to research, preparation, or pursuit.
