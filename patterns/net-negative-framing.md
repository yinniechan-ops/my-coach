# Pattern: Net Negative Framing

**First observed:** 2026-04-16 (session 2)

## Description

Yinnie holds a belief that if her entrepreneurial effort does not succeed, she will be "net negative" - that the years of work without conventional career advancement, the lower salary, and the sacrifice of rest and time will leave her worse off than peers who took conventional paths. This is not an occasional frustration thought; it is described as a longstanding frame ("for as long as I can remember") that has sharpened over the past two and a half years of sustained effort.

## Examples from Sessions

- **2026-04-16:** "Unless I succeed in being an entrepreneur, I just end up being that person who didn't have a life because I've been working all the time trying to make it work, and it didn't end up working. And my career is not excelling. So I'm net negative."
- **2026-04-18:** Not stated explicitly, but structurally present in the draw toward the cofounder prospect. Pull toward having a building partner after an exhausting solo path reads in part as an exit from the net negative trajectory.
- **2026-04-21:** Direct activation: "I've been working so hard for the past 2 years for absolutely no results." When challenged, she sharpened the claim: "No results means no users, no point in having a product no one uses." New element: she pushed back on the reframe explicitly - "I don't want you to glaze this over." This is different from prior sessions where she would receive challenge and partially acknowledge it.

## Clinical Notes

This frame is load-bearing. It means every day that passes without entrepreneurial success is experienced as a day that makes her final position worse. Every peer success is further evidence. Every slow data point from the app confirms the trajectory. It makes routine setbacks feel existential.

The frame is factually miscalibrated. Yinnie has relocated internationally twice, left a toxic role, shipped two working applications while employed full-time, and sustained a focused effort for nearly three years. These are not the actions or outputs of someone in a net negative position.

This pattern interacts with dispositional attribution: obstacles are not situational problems to solve, they are confirmations of the net negative trajectory. It also feeds the compounding anxiety loop: each setback does not discharge but accumulates as evidence of the prediction coming true.

**Session 6 update:** Simple counter-evidence no longer suffices. She is now actively resisting reframes she experiences as positive glossing. This may reflect the belief hardening under sustained pressure, or it may reflect a more honest engagement - she is distinguishing between the real distribution problem (which is genuine and should not be minimised) and the net negative narrative on top of it. The coaching challenge has shifted: the useful intervention is not "you have results" (she will reject this) but rather precision about the specific, solvable problem. "What does this feedback tell you about who you're targeting?" rather than "you've done well."

Behavioural evidence (an actual user, a genuine piece of feedback, one thing working) is more likely to move this belief than reframing. The Reddit feedback - people saying "no use for it" - is data that, if examined carefully, could become this kind of concrete evidence. She has not examined it yet.

## Watch For

Any time she frames a setback as confirming she is "behind" or that others are "ahead." The comparison to specific peers is this pattern in action. Name the frame, then go to the specific problem rather than the counter-argument. Also watch for decisions being made from exhaustion and the desire for an exit - particularly around partnership and co-building, which she has named as what she most wants from peers who appear to have it. Watch for "I don't want you to glaze this over" or similar language indicating the reframe is being experienced as dismissal rather than challenge.
