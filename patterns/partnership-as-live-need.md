# Pattern: Partnership as Live Need

**First observed:** 2026-04-19 (session 5, background)
**Named explicitly:** 2026-04-26 (session 7)
**Origin event disclosed:** 2026-04-29 (session 8a)

## Description

Yinnie has been building solo for nearly three years. The need for a cofounder or building partner has moved from a background preference to a central, explicitly articulated need. When asked what she wants, partnership consistently surfaces first.

## Origin Event

Disclosed in session 8 for the first time. Her ex-cofounder: they worked together for "a couple of good months," were about to launch (app store ready), he accidentally deleted their waitlist on a Supabase free trial, showed no remorse, then admitted he wasn't invested in the idea. She described it as "very devastating" and then said: "I tried to do it on my own. I haven't really cracked it."

This loss was both practical (product setback at the point of launch) and relational (betrayal of commitment - he wasn't as invested as she was, didn't care when things went wrong). It has never been processed as a loss. She moved straight to solo building.

## Examples from Sessions

- **2026-04-21 (session 6):** Named partnership first when discussing what Mimi has that she wants.
- **2026-04-26 (session 7):** Full articulation: accountability, sparring, companionship, different perspective.
- **2026-04-29 (session 8a):** Ex-cofounder story disclosed. When asked what she brings, she said: "I don't think people want to work with me because I honestly don't bring anything other than my pure grit." The self-assessment has a specific origin: someone demonstrably didn't care about working with her.

## Clinical Notes

The ex-cofounder loss shapes every subsequent partnership prospect:
- Pre-calculation of failure ("she's probably not going to respond") maps onto the expectation that commitment won't be reciprocated.
- "I don't bring anything" maps onto the experience of a partner who wasn't invested in the idea she was building.
- Suspicion of others' stated commitment ("people say what they wanna say, and then they act differently") is directly from the cofounder experience.

The "I don't bring anything other than pure grit" self-assessment is the deepest version of the net negative framing applied to partnership. Her actual track record (two shipped apps, three years of sustained effort, international relocations) directly contradicts this. The coach challenged it in session 8. Whether it landed is unclear.

The need for partnership also parallels the Cyrus material. The October incident centrally involves someone who was supposed to be her partner failing to show up. The cofounder who walked away was also someone who was supposed to be her partner and didn't show up. The pattern across domains: she commits fully, the other person does not match her commitment, she is left carrying it alone.

## Watch For

- Rachel coffee outcome. If it went well, watch for over-investment before reciprocal signals.
- If it didn't go well, watch for "I don't bring anything" activating.
- Whether the ex-cofounder loss gets processed further or stays as a one-time disclosure.
- The cross-domain pattern: full commitment from her, inadequate reciprocation from partners (both romantic and professional).
- "People say what they wanna say, and then they act differently" - genuine caution that also functions as preemptive self-protection.
