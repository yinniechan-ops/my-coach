# Pattern: Partnership as Live Need

**First observed:** 2026-04-19 (session 5, background)
**Named explicitly:** 2026-04-26 (session 7)
**Origin event disclosed:** 2026-04-29 (session 8a)
**Current state:** Stalled (session 10)

## Description

Yinnie has been building solo for nearly three years. The need for a cofounder or building partner has moved from a background preference to a central, explicitly articulated need. When asked what she wants, partnership consistently surfaces first.

## Origin Event

Disclosed in session 8 for the first time. Her ex-cofounder: they worked together for "a couple of good months," were about to launch (app store ready), he accidentally deleted their waitlist on a Supabase free trial, showed no remorse, then admitted he wasn't invested. She described it as "very devastating" and then said: "I tried to do it on my own. I haven't really cracked it."

This loss was both practical (product setback at the point of launch) and relational (betrayal of commitment - he wasn't as invested as she was, didn't care when things went wrong). It has never been processed as a loss. She moved straight to solo building.

## Current State (Session 10)

Rachel prospect is effectively closed. Coffee happened, energy was lukewarm, Rachel is hedging (meeting other people, may take a break). Yinnie's read is grounded - not pre-calculating rejection, reading the actual signal. She is pocketing it unless Rachel re-initiates.

This leaves her with no live partnership prospect for the first time since session 7. When probed, the "I don't bring anything" feeling activated. She did not spiral but also did not engage. The flat delivery ("it's fine, we just move forward") is the knowledge-feeling gap: composed response delivered without conviction.

The risk: with no prospect in play and mood worsening, she may stop looking. The need has not diminished - the energy to pursue it may have.

## Clinical Notes

The ex-cofounder loss shapes every subsequent partnership prospect:
- Pre-calculation of failure maps onto the expectation that commitment won't be reciprocated.
- "I don't bring anything" maps onto the experience of a partner who wasn't invested.
- Suspicion of others' stated commitment is directly from the cofounder experience.

The cross-domain pattern of absorbing relational costs (accommodation-resentment) also applies: she committed fully to the ex-cofounder, he didn't match, she carried it alone. Same structure as Cyrus and the workplace.

## Watch For

- Whether she is actively looking for new cofounder prospects or has stopped.
- Whether the "I don't bring anything" belief hardens in the absence of a live prospect to test it against.
- Whether the mood decline reduces her capacity to pursue partnership.
- Whether she frames the Rachel outcome as confirmation of her inadequacy or as a neutral mismatch.
- The YC cofounder matching platform - is she still on it? Engaging?
