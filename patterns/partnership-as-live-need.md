# Pattern: Partnership as Live Need

**First observed:** 2026-04-19 (session 5, background)
**Named explicitly:** 2026-04-26 (session 7)

## Description

Yinnie has been building solo for nearly three years. The need for a cofounder or building partner has moved from a background preference to a central, explicitly articulated need. When asked what she wants, partnership consistently surfaces first - before speed, before results, before the idea itself.

## Examples from Sessions

- **2026-04-19 (session 5):** Cofounder prospect closed with minimal processing. The loss of the prospect was not examined.
- **2026-04-21 (session 6):** When asked what Mimi has that she wants, she named partnership first, ahead of speed and results. She also named jealousy strong enough to affect her capacity to be a good friend.
- **2026-04-26 (session 7):** When asked directly why she wants a cofounder, she gave the most articulated answer across all sessions: "accountability partner, someone like a sparring partner. Companionship or someone who goes through it with you. I think it makes it much easier and then much more enjoyable to have a different perspective as well." She also said the exhaustion is not only about Daily failing but about doing everything alone.

## Clinical Notes

This is not simply a business strategy preference. Three years of solo building while employed full-time, with a cofounder who left (circumstances still unexplored), has depleted her at a level that intersects with the chronic low mood. The loneliness of solo building is a maintaining factor in the exhaustion and possibly in the depression.

The need for partnership interacts with several other patterns:
- **Net negative framing:** Peers who have partners (Mimi) appear to succeed faster, confirming the sense of falling behind.
- **Dispositional attribution:** When cofounder calls go poorly (Rachel), she routes it through personal failure rather than situational factors.
- **Knowledge-feeling gap:** She knows she wants partnership, knows what it would give her, and still pre-calculates rejection before pursuing it.

The Rachel prospect is the first concrete opportunity in this space since the cofounder closure in session 4. How she handles this - whether she can show up to a coffee prepared and present, and whether she can tolerate the uncertainty of someone else's timeline - will be informative.

## Watch For

- Whether the Rachel coffee happens and how she shows up to it.
- Whether she over-invests in the prospect before receiving reciprocal signals (the opposite risk from under-investing).
- Whether the original cofounder departure is ever processed. This may be a significant unexamined loss.
- Whether partnership needs are being expressed through the Cyrus relationship material as well - the October incident centrally involves someone who was supposed to be her partner failing to show up.
- "I don't want to appease" and "I only want to join someone on equal footing" - genuine values that may also function as protection against vulnerability.
