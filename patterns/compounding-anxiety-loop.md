# Pattern: Compounding Anxiety Loop

**First observed:** 2026-03-27 (session 1)

## Description

Yinnie explicitly described a mechanism in which anxiety from one episode is not discharged but carried forward and amplified into the next similar situation. She used the phrase "the replay then feeds into the next scenario" and "it will just amplify that and continue that negative loop."

## Examples from Sessions

- **2026-03-27:** Meeting self-doubt - she did not speak, then replayed the meeting afterwards, and described that replay as raising the anxiety baseline for the next meeting.

## Clinical Notes

She is aware of the mechanism and can describe it clearly. She is not aware of any break point in it - she described the replay as "constant." The clinical question is whether the break point is (a) interrupting the in-session spiral before it sets, (b) interrupting the replay afterwards, or (c) something structural that prevents the load from building. Not yet enough data to know which is most tractable. Keep tracking the compounding over time - if she reports the anxiety level increasing across weeks, this pattern is accelerating and needs prioritising.

## Watch For

Ask about replay duration and frequency when she brings examples. Track whether the baseline anxiety level reported session to session is stable, rising, or falling.
