# Pattern: Compounding Anxiety Loop

**First observed:** 2026-03-27 (session 1)

## Description

Yinnie explicitly described a mechanism in which anxiety from one episode is not discharged but carried forward and amplified into the next similar situation. She used the phrase "the replay then feeds into the next scenario" and "it will just amplify that and continue that negative loop."

## Examples from Sessions

- **2026-03-27:** Meeting self-doubt - she did not speak, then replayed the meeting afterwards, and described that replay as raising the anxiety baseline for the next meeting.
- **2026-04-16:** The loop appears to have shifted from episodic to baseline. She is no longer describing specific replay events - she describes a persistent low state: "every morning" when she wakes up, flatness that has been "building for a while." Two and a half years of compounding may have raised the floor of the anxiety level to a chronic state consistent with the depression picture.

## Clinical Notes

In session 1, the question was whether the break point was interrupting the in-session spiral, interrupting the replay, or something structural. Session 2 suggests the compounding has progressed beyond episodic spirals into a chronic baseline low mood. This changes the clinical picture: the loop may no longer be the primary intervention target - it may now be maintained by the depression, which requires its own treatment pathway (therapy, which is in process).

The net negative framing pattern interacts with this loop: each setback is not just anxiety-provoking but confirmatory of a feared identity outcome, which prevents discharge.

## Watch For

Ask about whether the baseline mood has changed session to session - is it stable, rising, or falling? If she reports it worsening, this pattern is accelerating and the therapy pathway needs to be prioritised. Track whether specific episodic spirals are still occurring or whether the presentation has moved fully to chronic flatness.
