# Pattern: Dispositional Attribution

**First observed:** 2026-03-27 (session 1)
**Origin event identified:** 2026-04-29 (session 8a)

## Description

When Yinnie hits a real obstacle or feels blocked, she routes the explanation through personal failure - character, willingness, or identity - rather than situational factors or specific skill gaps. The attribution is internal and global, not external or specific.

Domain-specific: most active in professional and entrepreneurial contexts where external confirmation is absent. Quieter in interpersonal contexts where the other person's behaviour is clearly the relevant factor.

## Origin Event

Session 8 revealed a likely origin: her ex-cofounder was demonstrably not invested, deleted their waitlist without remorse, and admitted he didn't care. Rather than processing this as his failure of commitment, the experience has become a generalised expectation: "I don't think people want to work with me because I honestly don't bring anything other than my pure grit." Someone else's failure of investment has been internalised as evidence of her own inadequacy.

## Examples from Sessions

- **2026-03-27:** Cannot get users - concludes she does not want it badly enough.
- **2026-03-27:** Will not post on LinkedIn - frames as character flaw.
- **2026-04-16:** Low sign-up rate becomes "maybe it's just not the right thing" and "should I give up."
- **2026-04-18:** Cofounder ambiguity routes to "maybe it's not me."
- **2026-04-26:** Rachel call: "I didn't sell myself," "I fumbled it" - routing through personal failure rather than situational factors.
- **2026-04-29:** "I'm playing all my cards wrong" about salary comparison. "I don't bring anything other than pure grit" about partnership value. These are the deepest activations yet - from "I'm behind" to "I have nothing to offer."

## Quieter Instances

- **2026-04-19 (session 4):** Attributed Cyrus's behaviour to Cyrus, appropriately.
- **2026-04-19 (session 5):** Handled cofounder closure without self-blame.

## Clinical Notes

The pattern has deepened across sessions. Early sessions showed specific-situation attributions ("I didn't speak up" becomes "I don't know what I'm doing"). Session 8 showed a global self-assessment ("I don't bring anything") that functions as a stable belief rather than a momentary distortion. This is harder to challenge because it feels to her like realism rather than distortion.

The ex-cofounder origin is clinically significant: someone else's failure has been internalised as her deficiency. The reframe that might land is not "you have lots to offer" (she will experience this as glossing) but something more specific: "Your last cofounder wasn't invested. That's about him, not about what you bring. You kept building for three years after he left. That's the evidence of what you bring."

The domain-specificity remains: most active when external confirmation is absent, quieter when the other person's behaviour is clearly the variable.

## Watch For

- "I don't bring anything" or variants - this is the deepest activation.
- Jumping from specific obstacle to global self-verdict.
- Whether the Rachel coffee outcome triggers the pattern or not.
- Whether she can hold the ex-cofounder loss as his failure rather than her inadequacy.
- "Playing my cards wrong" - this blends dispositional and strategic, worth unpacking if it recurs.
