# Pattern: Dispositional Attribution

**First observed:** 2026-03-27 (session 1)

## Description

When Yinnie hits a real obstacle or feels blocked, she routes the explanation through personal failure - character, willingness, or identity - rather than situational factors or specific skill gaps. The attribution is internal and global, not external or specific.

## Examples from Sessions

- **2026-03-27:** Cannot get users for her apps - concludes she does not want it badly enough and is "the person stopping herself." Actual obstacle: one specific distribution method (public content creation) conflicts with her social anxiety and preference for privacy.
- **2026-03-27:** Will not post on LinkedIn - frames this as a character flaw ("I can't even post fucking my content") rather than a specific behaviour that is hard for a specific reason.
- **2026-03-27:** Did not ask questions in a meeting - concludes she does not know what she is doing, rather than: she was anxious about how a specific question would land.
- **2026-04-16:** Low sign-up rate from 50 invites became "maybe it's just not the right thing" and "should I give up" - jumping from a specific data point to a global conclusion about the product and her judgment. Partially interrupted when the actual data was examined: two distribution methods tried, neither targeting the right audience.
- **2026-04-18:** Ambiguity from a cofounder prospect routed immediately to "maybe it's not me" and "should I just take the hint." Actual data: the prospect had asked to be kept updated ten minutes prior. Pattern interrupted when this was pointed out.

## Clinical Notes

She is partially aware of this pattern - she catches herself mid-sentence at times. However, awareness does not interrupt it. The reframe that lands best is precision: pointing to her actual track record, or examining the specific obstacle rather than accepting the global attribution. She engages with evidence-based challenge more readily than reassurance.

In session 2 she accepted the challenge ("you've tried two things, not many") without significant resistance. In session 3 the same dynamic appeared in an interpersonal context (the cofounder situation) and was interrupted in the same way - by pointing at concrete evidence rather than offering reassurance. The pattern is intact but appears responsive to precise, evidence-based pushback.

The pattern is particularly active in ambiguous situations where the other party has not explicitly confirmed interest. Absence of confirmation is read as implicit rejection, which is then attributed to personal inadequacy.

## Watch For

Any time she uses "I just can't" or "I'm not willing" language around a specific behaviour, or jumps from one data point to a verdict about herself or the product. Test whether she has actually tried alternatives or whether the conclusion is drawn from one blocked path. In interpersonal situations: watch for "taking the hint" when no clear hint exists.
