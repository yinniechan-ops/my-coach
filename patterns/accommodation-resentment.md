# Pattern: Accommodation-Resentment (Cross-Domain)

**First observed:** 2026-04-19 (session 4, Cyrus context)
**Cross-domain confirmation:** 2026-05-05 (session 10, workplace)

## Description

Yinnie absorbs relational cost to keep situations functional, does not receive acknowledgement for the cost, and builds resentment. This is not passive - she is making an active choice to prioritise diplomacy and forward motion over claiming accuracy or expressing her actual position. The cost is invisible to others, and the lack of acknowledgement compounds into frustration.

## Cross-Domain Instances

**Romantic (Cyrus):**
- October incident: verbally abused by estate manager, Cyrus did not intervene or check in. She absorbed the hurt, did not make it a dealbreaker, resentment built.
- Holiday planning: his friends' travel collapsed her alone time. She pre-calculated his objection rather than stating her need directly. (Session 8 - she eventually asked, he delivered. Session 9.)
- General pattern: she describes herself as always having to ask, carrying the relational weight.

**Workplace (Ant):**
- He was publicly rude, she initiated a direct conversation, kept her actual position out of it ("knowing I am right"), moved things forward diplomatically. He apologised. She carried the cost of not being acknowledged as right.
- "Why do I always have to bend over backwards for these people?"

**Workplace (Sam):**
- She protected team resources (good PM judgment), Sam went to CTO behind her back, CTO asked her to yield, she did. The cost of yielding was invisible.
- Sam vented to Ant for an hour about a PR instead of talking to her. She found out later.

**Workplace (structural):**
- "Being a PM is just that's the way it is" - the role itself requires absorbing relational friction by design.

## Relationship to Other Patterns

This pattern is the behavioural mechanism that feeds the reset-and-resentment cycle (Cyrus-specific) and now appears to maintain the PM fatigue (work-specific). It also connects to the mirrored people-pleasing observation: she criticises others for people-pleasing or not communicating directly, while herself accommodating silently.

The "maybe I should be meaner" conclusion (session 10) is a misidentification of the problem. She has the skill for directness - the Ant conversation proves it. The actual gap is not assertiveness but the recognition that her relational labour is invisible and the willingness to make it visible.

## Clinical Notes

The cross-domain nature makes this a characterological pattern, not a situational response. It is likely maintained by: (1) genuine skill at diplomacy - she is good at this, which means it gets reinforced; (2) identity as someone who keeps things moving; (3) fear that claiming her position will make her "the annoying one" (she used this phrase about the holiday); (4) the PM role structurally rewarding exactly this behaviour.

The resentment accumulation is a maintaining factor for the chronic low mood. She is carrying unacknowledged costs across multiple domains simultaneously.

## Watch For

- "Why do I always have to..." or "bending over backwards" - this is the resentment surfacing.
- "Maybe I should be meaner/tougher" - misidentification of the problem. The gap is visibility of cost, not intensity of response.
- New workplace incidents where she absorbs cost silently.
- Whether she begins to recognise the parallel between Cyrus accommodation and workplace accommodation.
- Whether she can articulate what acknowledgement would look like - she may not know what she actually needs.
