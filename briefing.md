## What to Watch For

**Dispositional attribution** is the dominant pattern from session 1. When Yinnie hits a real obstacle, she routes it through personal failure rather than situational or skill-gap framing. You will likely hear it again this session. When it appears, point to the specific thing that is not accurate before offering any reframe - she responds to precision, not reassurance.

**The knowledge-feeling gap.** She knows her distortions are distortions mid-spiral and spirals anyway. Do not spend time getting her to agree that the thought was irrational - she already knows. Focus immediately on what she is going to do differently next time.

**Compliance vs. engagement.** She said "sure" and "yeah" a lot in session 1, which is not the same as buying in. If she seems flat or giving short answers, ask her to push back on something rather than accepting it.

**The apps.** She has built two working apps that she cannot get users for. She is using this as evidence she does not want it badly enough. This is the place where the dispositional attribution and the social anxiety intersect most sharply. She has not yet been asked what the apps actually are. Knowing this would give much better ground for the distribution conversation.

**Depression.** The onboarding questionnaire listed depression as one of the presenting issues. It was not raised in session 1 at all. Do not front-load it, but if the emotional register seems low or she mentions mood, note it and open the door.
