## Themes

**1. Mimi as net negative framing trigger.**
Yinnie opened with a direct peer comparison: friend from Shanghai launched a podcast with a co-host in one month, already has listeners. Her immediate framing: "I've been working so hard for the past 2 years for absolutely no results." The net negative pattern activated before the session properly started. When challenged on "no results," she sharpened the claim rather than accepting the reframe: "no results means no users, no point in having a product no one uses." She pushed back on the coaching response directly - "I don't want you to glaze this over" - which is more resistant than in previous sessions. She is not entirely wrong: the distribution problem is real. The distortion is the leap from "no users yet" to "two years, no results at all."

**2. The Mimi comparison reveals what she is missing.**
When asked what specifically she wanted that Mimi has, she escalated to "everything - all of it," then landed with honesty: "I am very jealous. So much so that I find myself not being able to copy which is not the kind of partner I want to be." When pressed, she named three things: speed, partnership, results. The partnership element is the most clinically significant. Mimi did not build something impressive; she found someone to build with and together they made something work quickly. That is what Yinnie does not have.

**3. Cyrus question asked - answer withheld.**
She confirmed she asked him. Then: "I didn't think I don't wanna discuss that right now." Firm, flat, brief. Not the usual redirect or social smoothing - an explicit refusal. This is new behaviour. Possible readings: the answer was difficult and she has not processed it; the answer was good and she does not yet trust it; she is holding it privately and it is not ready for the coaching space; or the Mimi spiral was simply more immediately live. The refusal was accepted in the moment. This cannot stay unaddressed at session 7.

**4. Real product feedback - first named in six sessions.**
Reddit replies received. Most people do not reply; those who do say they have no use for Daily. She framed this as confirmation of failure. It is actually the first substantive user feedback named across all sessions - positioning data, not a verdict. The session was cut before she could say what reasons people gave. This is the most immediately actionable thread.

**5. Therapy insurance still pending.**
She flagged it herself when asked: "Nope. Not yet. That's the process I cannot control." Brief, slightly resigned.

**6. Session ended without a commitment.**
The transcript cuts off mid-exchange. No commitment placed. Most incomplete session ending so far.
