## Themes

She continued alone and "hasn't really cracked it." The loss was both practical (product setback) and relational (betrayal of commitment).

**2. "I don't bring anything other than pure grit" - deepest self-assessment.**
Goes beneath the net negative framing. Not "I'm behind" but "I have nothing to offer." She framed it as explaining why people don't want to work with her, calling it "not a very sellable quality." The coach challenged with her track record (two shipped apps, international relocations, three years of sustained effort). Whether it landed is unclear.

Second specific peer comparison in two sessions - a different friend from Mimi, now in final rounds for a YC-backed startup at 130-150k. The pattern is generalising: not one trigger person but a sense of everyone moving while she is stuck. She resisted the coach's attempt to separate the comparison more forcefully than before.

**4. Cyrus material finally arrived with more nuance than expected.**
His answer to the daily changes question: "I'll be there for you more" - vague and disappointing. But the incidents she described show small changes: he stood up at the glasses shop (she noted he wouldn't have done this before), got her water at the cinema, tried to carve out alone time on the holiday. The most significant development: she recognised she is on "high alert" and may be watching for evidence of failure rather than assessing fairly. First time across all sessions she has questioned her own read on Cyrus.

**5. Holiday conflict as live test of direct communication.**
She has a specific want (keep the original day alone) and has not asked for it. She is pre-calculating his objection ("it would make his friends feel unwelcome"). The coach named this as carrying a conversation in her head that hasn't happened yet and losing it before it starts.

**6. People-pleasing as Cyrus's core structural concern.**
She identified his people-pleasing as the deepest worry - not individual incidents but the trait. He has said he "doesn't understand why people-pleasing is a bad thing." She is also pre-calculating how to phrase her own request to avoid making his friends uncomfortable - a parallel she has not noticed.

**7. Rachel coffee confirmed but no preparation done.**
Rachel responded. Coffee scheduled for April 30. Yinnie did zero research, citing no time and pessimism ("what's the point"). The coach set a 30-minute research commitment. Unknown whether she followed through.
