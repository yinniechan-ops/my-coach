## Themes

**1. Workplace relational cost as a cross-domain pattern.**
Yinnie brought two workplace narratives for the first time in ten sessions. Ant (senior developer) was publicly rude in a meeting, Yinnie initiated a direct one-on-one the next day, he apologised, it resolved. Sam (most junior) went behind her back twice (to CTO about scope, vented to Ant about a PR review for an hour) rather than communicating with her directly. Sam has since quit. Seeing him at team drinks tonight reactivated the frustration.

The deeper signal: in both cases, Yinnie absorbed relational cost to keep things functional. With Ant, she knew she was right (scope was agreed, walkthroughs happened, the objection should have been raised earlier) but chose diplomacy over claiming accuracy. With Sam, she protected team resources for good reasons but yielded when the CTO asked. In neither case did anyone acknowledge the cost she carried. She arrived at: "Why do I always have to bend over backwards for these people?"

This is structurally identical to the Cyrus dynamic. She does the relational work, others do not see it, resentment builds. The pattern is cross-domain, not relationship-specific.

**2. The "maybe I should be meaner" slide.**
She went from "I feel innocent and unfairly treated" to "maybe I need to be more like **** you" in seconds. The coach caught it. Her reference point is Luke (another PM) who "wouldn't really give a ****." But the actual evidence from her own narrative contradicts the need to be meaner - the Ant conversation resolved precisely because she was direct without aggression. The real issue is not insufficient assertiveness but unacknowledged relational labour.

**3. PM fatigue as a structural maintaining factor.**
She identified the PM role itself as part of the exhaustion: "being a PM is just that's the way it is." When asked whether she wants to change the role or leave it, she said those are the same thing. The role requires her to absorb exactly the kind of relational cost that depletes her most. This makes the holding pattern actively harmful, not just unsatisfying.

**4. Rachel - closed without processing.**
Coffee happened. Rachel was lukewarm - meeting other people, potentially taking a break, will continue only if design partners return good data. Yinnie told Rachel she was interested. No reciprocal energy. Yinnie is pocketing it unless Rachel re-initiates. This is a grounded read - not pre-calculating rejection, reading the actual signal. But the "I don't bring anything" feeling activated when the coach probed. She has no live partnership prospect for the first time since session 7.

**5. Mood at its most concerning.**
"Dead on the inside, which is not untrue." A colleague noticed unprompted ("you used to be so choppy"). Therapy is "still in progress" - no timeline detail. She could not identify a single thing that would make the week lighter. Exit was the flattest of any session. This is not the episodic depletion of sessions 7-8 - this reads as the chronic low mood deepening.
