## Themes

**1. Commitments completed - brief acknowledgement, then pivot.**
Both outstanding commitments (cofounder message and Reddit reply) were confirmed done at the open. The coach noted the cofounder response should be revisited, but the session moved immediately to the relationship topic before that happened. The response from the cofounder prospect is now an untracked thread. The two-session-old Reddit commitment being done is meaningful - worth noting without overstating.

**2. October incident - serious, genuine, unresolved.**
The core presenting material. Cyrus stood approximately ten steps away while Yinnie was verbally abused by a building manager, said nothing, and did not check in with her afterwards. She cried that evening and nearly ended the relationship. They reconciled, but she describes the reconciliation as "playing against her instincts" and "crossing her fingers." She did not resolve it; she agreed to move forward. The emotional weight has not discharged. When she told her brother about it months later, the feelings were as strong or stronger - including embarrassment at still being with him.

**3. The reset pattern - she is papering over the conflict herself.**
The most clinically significant moment: she identified unprompted that she keeps accepting the reset to normal life (dinner, TV, cuddles), which teaches Cyrus that the upset passes without requiring genuine change. This is her own formulation, arrived at without prompting. She is not passive in this dynamic - she is actively resolving the tension herself, then resenting him for not changing.

**4. "I don't know" as the ceiling of his response.**
She explicitly told him a month ago that "I don't know" is not a sufficient answer. Last night, when she was on the sofa and he woke up, his response this morning was again "I don't know what was going on." She reads this as a pattern. Whether this is a habit he has not broken or the actual limit of his self-awareness is genuinely unclear from available data, but it is a legitimate concern.

**5. Her data is real but she is reading it through a contaminated lens.**
She went to holiday planning as a data point against him and then partly let him off by attributing it to his friends. She could not name a single clear positive data point in his favour in the past month - but immediately when prompted, named several consistent positives (dishes, always travels to her, little things). Both are true: he is not useless, and he has not yet demonstrated the specific capacity she needs.

**6. This relationship is carrying additional load given the wider clinical picture.**
She is already exhausted, carrying chronic low mood, running on depleted reserves. A relationship requiring daily vigilance and unresolved hurt adds to that weight. Not a coaching concern to manage clinically, but relevant context.
