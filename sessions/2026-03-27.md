## Themes

**1. Therapy fatigue and the demand for forward motion.**
Yinnie has been in therapy and found it unhelpful - not because she lacks self-awareness, but precisely because she has it. She can name her patterns. What she cannot do is interrupt them. She is looking for intervention, not excavation.

**2. The knowledge-feeling gap.**
In the meeting example, she knew mid-spiral that the concern was irrational ("they already know I'm not technical") and spiralled anyway. This is the core clinical site: accurate knowledge does not interrupt the anxiety loop. The work has to happen at the level of behaviour and real-time cognition, not insight.

**3. Self-blame as the interpretive default.**
The most consistent pattern across the session: when she hits a genuine obstacle, she routes it through personal failure. Cannot get users for the apps - she is not willing enough. Will not post on LinkedIn - she is making excuses. Did not speak in the meeting - she does not know what she is doing. The attribution is always internal and dispositional, never situational or specific.

**4. The business ambition as a daily wound.**
The day job is not neutral. It is experienced as daily confirmation that she has not become what she wants to be. This is load-bearing for mood and not yet explored.

**5. Social anxiety as the blocker, not character.**
She resists the label at first ("I know it's easy to just label it") but accepts it when it is pointed at a concrete behaviour (not posting content). Distribution is not a knowledge gap - she knows one path and will not take it. That is the anxiety, not laziness.

## Emotional Register

Started flat and guarded - short answers, a lot of "yeah" and "sure." Opened up meaningfully when talking about the meeting example, which she had clearly replayed many times. Most animated and distressed when discussing the apps and distribution - the "I can't even post fucking my content" moment was the session's emotional peak, and the most unguarded she got. Returned to flat-ish compliance by the end. No evident breakthrough, but genuine engagement in the middle section. Surface-establishing session, as expected - foundation built, not depth reached.

## Claims Extracted

- "Every day" - catastrophising and self-doubt are daily occurrences, not episodic.
- She has built two working apps that are "fully functioning."
- She cannot get users for the apps.
- She identifies content creation / LinkedIn posting as the known distribution path and refuses it because of how it would feel to be that visible.
- She says she "doesn't like being that well known" - presented as preference, worth testing whether this is value or avoidance.
- She replays meetings constantly, and the replay compounds the next episode.
- She attributes not posting to not wanting it badly enough - holds this as evidence of character failure.
- Previous therapy felt like an "endless loop" with no concrete outcome.
- She said she wants to talk "every day" if it would help - noteworthy motivation level, possibly also dependency-seeking.

## Contradictions

1. She says she will "do anything" to build her business, then immediately uses "can't" language around the one distribution path she knows. She caught this herself mid-sentence - it is live for her, not unconscious.

2. She presents as someone who makes excuses but her actual history - relocating internationally twice, leaving Holland and Barrett, shipping two apps - is the opposite profile. The self-narrative is badly miscalibrated against the evidence.

3. She said therapy did not help because she "never got better" but also framed it as therapists asking her to find her own answers. These are slightly different complaints: no progress vs. wrong method. Worth clarifying later whether she means she did not feel better, or she did not change her behaviour.

## Patterns

- **Dispositional attribution** (new): When blocked, defaults to "I am the problem" rather than "this is a solvable external constraint." See patterns/dispositional-attribution.md.
- **Compounding anxiety loop** (new): Explicitly described - replays compound across episodes, raising the anxiety baseline for the next similar situation. See patterns/compounding-anxiety-loop.md.
- **Knowledge-feeling gap** (new): Can identify a cognitive distortion mid-spiral but cannot interrupt it. Self-awareness is not protective. See patterns/knowledge-feeling-gap.md.
