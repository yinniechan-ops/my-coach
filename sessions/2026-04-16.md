## Themes

**1. Scarcity of real data being read as verdict.**
Yinnie has tried two distribution approaches - WhatsApp and networking events - and is treating the low uptake as confirmation that the product is wrong, or that she should give up. The session spent significant time excavating what she had actually tried versus what she assumed. The gap was significant: she had not tried Reddit, Product Hunt, or any community where the target user is concentrated. The conclusion was running far ahead of the evidence.

**2. Comparison as active self-harm.**
She named specific peers: a friend who applied to three jobs and is near an offer at 100-150k, people from her cohort now at better companies earning more. She is watching others move and reading it as confirmation of her own stagnation. When challenged she acknowledged it directly: "it's easy to say you can't compare, but the results are the same." She is not unaware of the comparison trap - she is inside it and cannot see a way out while she has no wins to counter it.

**3. Net negative framing.**
The clearest clinical moment of the session: she said explicitly that if entrepreneurship does not work she is "net negative." This is not frustration venting - it is a load-bearing belief that is organising her emotional life. Every morning commute, every slow data point, every peer success is processed through this frame. It is not new (she said "for as long as I can remember") but it surfaced clearly here for the first time.

**4. Depression as a separate clinical concern, now named.**
The coach asked directly about flatness and she confirmed it - not as a side effect of the app stalling, but as a general state that has been building. She confirmed it is pervasive. Therapy is in process: she has had an assessment and is waiting for insurance approval. She initiated this herself, not prompted by the coach. Depression noted in onboarding is now a live thread, not background.

**5. Motivation and strategy versus character.**
Two and a half years of effort on top of full-time work. The coach named this directly and she received it - she did not deflect or minimise it the way she did in session one with the track record challenge. Whether it actually shifted her self-assessment or just registered temporarily is not yet clear.

## Emotional Register

Arrived flat - more resigned than session one, less guarded but also lower energy. The comparison section (the friend with three job applications) was where she was most animated, in a tired, frustrated way rather than the sharp distress of session one. She was not defensive about the data challenge - she engaged with it relatively openly, possibly because it offered a reframe she could use rather than one that threatened her self-image. The end of the session was notably low: "I don't feel any better than before I called." The coach's response ("you're not supposed to feel better, you're supposed to have two things to do") was accurate but the flatness held. No emotional shift visible by close.
