## Themes

The prospective cofounder replied with something non-committal ("you were very clear, smiley face"), which Yinnie read as a polite no. She assessed the evidence, drew a conclusion, and moved on: "we move forward." No spiral, no self-blame, no routing through personal inadequacy. The dispositional attribution pattern was quiet. This is worth noting - it is meaningfully different from how ambiguity has landed in prior sessions. May reflect lower emotional stakes, or may indicate the pattern is more domain-specific than previously understood.

**2. The Cyrus conversation happened and was more substantive than anticipated.**

**3. The reset pattern operated in real time during the update call.**
Yinnie let "daily changes" go past without pressing. Her explanation: "the conversation went somewhere else." Her immediate follow-up: "I can always bring it up another time." When the coach named this as the reset pattern she identified last session, she initially accepted it, then pushed back with an argument: if he is genuinely making changes, he should not need to prepare, and she will ask him next weekend. The pushback had some substance. Whether it represents genuine conviction or deflection of accountability is not yet clear.

**4. She formulated the question clearly when pressed.**
Asked what she would say, she gave a precise, non-accusatory formulation unprompted: "You mentioned you're making daily changes to resolve what happened in October - can you tell me what those are?" This is a good question. She was not coached into the wording.

**5. Session ended before the evaluation criteria were established.**
The coach asked what a satisfying answer would look like. Yinnie said she did not know but "surprising in a good way." The session ended before this could be developed. She does not yet have a picture of what she is evaluating him against, which creates risk: she may accept a plausible-sounding answer that does not address the underlying concern, or miss a genuinely good one because she has not named what she is looking for.
