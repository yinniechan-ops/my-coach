Alongside the day job, for nearly three years, she has been building apps independently. She has shipped two working products, the most recent of which (Daily: an AI-powered personalised podcast aggregator) she shelved in session 7 after confirming it has no product-market fit.

Cyrus stood by and did not intervene or check in afterwards. This event is the defining injury. She is intelligent, self-aware, and analytically capable. She can name her patterns, locate cognitive distortions in real time, and engage with direct challenge without collapsing. She is increasingly assertive with the coach - she calls out errors, pushes back on repeated questions, and resists reframes she experiences as glossing. She is exhausted. The exhaustion has at least five identified sources: three years of solo effort on apps while employed full-time; the unprocessed cofounder loss; an unresolved relational injury; a PM role that structurally depletes her; and a chronic low mood she describes as lasting one to two years, which as of session 10 may be worsening ("dead on the inside," corroborated by a colleague noticing the change).

## Core Patterns

**Accommodation-resentment (cross-domain):** Yinnie absorbs relational cost to keep situations functional, receives no acknowledgement, and builds resentment. Previously observed only in the Cyrus context, confirmed in session 10 as cross-domain: two workplace instances (Ant, Sam) show the identical structure. The PM role structurally rewards this behaviour. She misidentifies the problem as insufficient assertiveness ("maybe I should be meaner") when the actual gap is the invisibility of her relational labour. The Ant conversation proves she has the directness skill; what she lacks is recognition of what it costs her and willingness to make that cost visible.

**Dispositional attribution:** When she hits an obstacle, the explanation routes through personal failure rather than situational factors. Domain-specific: most active in professional/entrepreneurial contexts where external confirmation is absent. The ex-cofounder loss is a likely origin: someone else's failure of investment has been internalised as her deficiency. The deepest activation is "I don't bring anything other than pure grit." Evidence-based challenge with specific data has more traction than reassurance.

**Net negative framing:** The deepest organising belief. If entrepreneurship does not work, she will be net negative. This has widened across sessions to multi-person comparisons and deepened from "I'm behind" to "I have nothing to offer." She actively resists reframes she experiences as positive glossing. Behavioural evidence (a partnership that works, a product someone uses) is more likely to move this than cognitive reframing. With the Rachel prospect closed, there is currently no behavioural evidence on the horizon to test this against.

**Knowledge-feeling gap:** She can identify a cognitive distortion mid-spiral and continue spiralling. Self-awareness is not protective. This extends to action: she can correctly analyse what she needs to do and still not do it. A variation observed in session 10: she delivers composed, "correct" responses ("it's fine, we just move forward") that she does not actually feel. Every intervention needs to sit at the level of concrete, pre-planned behaviour.

**Reset and resentment cycle (Cyrus-specific, currently in holding position):** Has evolved through three phases: premature reset, exhausting vigilance, and now an honest middle position ("he's someone who needs to be asked," "I don't like it but I can live with it"). The holiday is the next test. The self-questioning capacity ("am I projecting?") emerged in sessions 8-9 and represents genuine progress.

**Compounding anxiety loop / depression:** Has moved from episodic spirals to chronic baseline low mood lasting one to two years. As of session 10, the mood may be worsening: "dead on the inside," colleague noticed change, cannot identify anything helpful. This is the most urgent clinical thread. Therapy is in process but timeline unknown.

**Partnership as live need (currently stalled):** Three years of solo building has depleted her. The ex-cofounder loss is the origin event. Rachel prospect closed. No replacement. The risk is that declining mood reduces her capacity to look.

**Mirrored people-pleasing:** She criticises Cyrus's people-pleasing and Sam/Ant's indirect communication while herself accommodating silently to avoid conflict. Three instances across domains (Cyrus holiday, Ant, Sam/CTO). Not yet named to her. Part of the accommodation-resentment pattern.

## What the Coaching Relationship Is Trying to Do

She is not here for insight. She has insight. She is here for something to change.

The coaching relationship needs to: (1) stay close to behaviour - what specifically will she do differently, when, in response to what trigger; (2) test beliefs against evidence rather than offering reassurance; (3) hold the net negative framing and the "I don't bring anything" belief as the primary psychological obstacles; (4) not duplicate the therapy function while monitoring the depression carefully; (5) recognise that the accommodation-resentment pattern is now the widest-reaching pattern and connects the Cyrus material, the workplace material, and the PM fatigue.

The current risk is compound: the Rachel prospect has closed, the mood is worsening, the day job is experienced as actively draining, and there is no live behavioural evidence to counteract the "I don't bring anything" belief. The therapy timeline is the most clinically urgent unknown. The coach's role is to hold the entrepreneurial thread alive without pushing it when she is in crisis, and to help her see the cross-domain accommodation pattern clearly enough to begin making different choices.

She is assertive with the coach: she calls out errors, pushes back on repeated questions, and refuses reframes she experiences as glossing. This is healthy. The coach needs to earn the precision she expects.
