# Formulation

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21 (session 6)

## Who Yinnie Is

Yinnie is a product manager, likely in her late twenties or early thirties, living in a country she relocated to - twice internationally by her own account. She is working a full-time job (current salary 85k, experienced as below market) that she experiences as daily confirmation of not having become what she wants to be. Alongside this, for nearly three years, she has been building apps independently - she has two working products, the most recent of which (Daily: an AI-powered personalised podcast aggregator that surfaces newsletters and notifications based on individual workflow) she is actively trying to get users for. A cofounder was involved at some point and left; this has not been explored in depth. A second cofounder prospect was pursued across sessions 3-5 and closed after one non-committal reply.

She is in a relationship of approximately 18 months with a man named Cyrus. In October, during a house move, she was verbally abused by an estate manager. Cyrus stood by and did not intervene or check in with her afterwards. At session 6, she confirmed she had asked Cyrus directly about the daily changes he claimed to be making in relation to October. She would not discuss the answer in session. The relational thread is not closed.

She is intelligent, self-aware, and analytically capable. She can name her patterns, locate cognitive distortions in real time, and engage with direct challenge without collapsing - and, as of session 6, can push back on challenge she experiences as insufficient precision. She is also exhausted, and the exhaustion is not new. It has at least three sources: three years of solo effort on the apps while employed full-time; an unresolved relational injury; and the chronic low mood that has been building across both.

## What She Is Working On

The stated goal is distribution: getting real users for Daily, building something sustainable outside of employment. Underneath this is a larger question about identity and worth - whether she is the kind of person who can make something work, or whether she will be left behind by peers who took conventional paths.

As of session 6, real product feedback has arrived for the first time: Reddit replies indicating people have no use for Daily. This has not yet been examined. It is the first substantive signal and has not been treated as data - it has been treated as verdict.

Parallel to this, she is working out whether her relationship can hold. She has now asked the defining question. She has the answer. She has not yet brought it into the room.

Depression was noted in her clinical history and confirmed in session 2 as a persistent low mood and flatness building for some time. Therapy is in process: assessment completed, insurance approval pending. This is a separate clinical concern and should be held alongside the coaching work, not merged with it.

## Core Patterns

**Dispositional attribution:** When she hits a genuine obstacle, the explanation routes through personal failure or character rather than situational factors. The attribution is always internal and global. Her actual track record directly contradicts the self-assessment. Evidence-based challenge has more traction than reassurance. This pattern is domain-specific: most active in professional and entrepreneurial contexts where external confirmation is absent; quieter in interpersonal contexts where another person's behaviour is clearly the relevant factor.

**Net negative framing:** The deepest organising belief identified so far. If entrepreneurship does not work, she will be net negative - worse off than peers who took conventional paths, with nothing to show for the years of effort. This makes every setback existential and every peer success confirmatory. It is described as longstanding. As of session 6, she is actively resisting reframes of this belief, pushing back on positive glossing with more force than in prior sessions. This may reflect the belief hardening under sustained pressure, or it may reflect more honest engagement - she has learned to distinguish between the real distribution problem (which she will not let be glossed over) and the narrative layer on top of it. The coaching challenge has shifted: simple counter-evidence no longer suffices. She needs precision about what the specific, solvable problem actually is.

**Knowledge-feeling gap:** She can identify a cognitive distortion mid-spiral and continue spiralling anyway. Self-awareness is not protective. This extends to action: she can correctly analyse a situation and still not act on that analysis. Applies in both professional and relational domains. The intervention needs to sit at the level of concrete, pre-planned behaviour.

**Compounding anxiety loop:** Anxiety from one episode carries forward and amplifies the next. The October incident operates this way. The Mimi comparison has been sitting since Mimi's launch - not acute on the day. The overall picture has shifted from episodic to chronic baseline low mood, consistent with the depression picture.

**Reset and resentment cycle:** Specific to the relationship with Cyrus, possibly broader. She articulates the problem, they reset to normal life, he does not change, she resents him at the next trigger. She identified this mechanism herself. As of session 6: she has asked him the question and received an answer she is not sharing. The state of this pattern is currently unknown.

**Partnership as live need:** Emerging across sessions 5-6. She closed a cofounder prospect with minimal processing. In session 6, when asked what Mimi has that she wants, she named partnership first. Three years of solo building may have moved this from a preference to a felt need. This has not been directly addressed and may be a significant factor in the low mood and exhaustion.

## What the Coaching Relationship Is Trying to Do

She is not here for insight. She has insight. She is here for something to change. The explicit request from session 1 was for "concrete actions" - not reframing, not reflection, but specific behavioural interventions.

The coaching relationship therefore needs to: (1) stay close to behaviour - what specifically will she do differently, when, in response to what trigger; (2) test beliefs against evidence rather than offering reassurance - she receives precision better than warmth; (3) hold the net negative framing as the primary psychological obstacle, not the app distribution problem, which is a vehicle not the destination; (4) not duplicate the therapy function; (5) monitor the depression carefully and keep the therapy pathway moving.

As of session 6, a new constraint has emerged: she will push back on reframes she experiences as glossing over the real problem. This is not resistance to coaching - it is a signal that the coaching is being too reassuring and not precise enough. The response is not to back down from challenge but to aim the challenge more carefully: not "you have results" but "what specifically does this data tell you about the problem."

The relationship has expanded in scope to include relational material. The same operating principles apply. She does not need validation; she needs precision, specific behavioural guidance, and someone to hold her to what she already knows.

The risk in this relationship is compliance without conviction. Commitments need to be specific, small, and verified at the start of each subsequent session. A second risk has emerged: she is now capable of holding significant material (the Cyrus answer) outside the coaching space. When she withholds, the question is not what she is hiding but what is making it hard to bring in.
