# Formulation

**Last updated:** 2026-05-02 (session 8)

## Who Yinnie Is

Yinnie is a product manager, likely in her late twenties or early thirties, living in a country she relocated to - twice internationally by her own account. She is working a full-time job (current salary 85k, experienced as significantly below market compared to peers) that she experiences as daily confirmation of not having become what she wants to be. Alongside this, for nearly three years, she has been building apps independently. She has shipped two working products, the most recent of which (Daily: an AI-powered personalised podcast aggregator) she shelved in session 7 after confirming it has no product-market fit.

The cofounder story, disclosed for the first time in session 8, is central: they worked together for months, were about to launch, and he accidentally deleted their waitlist, showed no remorse, then admitted he wasn't invested. She continued alone. This loss - both practical and relational - has never been processed as a loss. It is the origin event for: the exhaustion, the pessimism about partnership, the "I don't think people want to work with me" belief, and the pre-calculation of failure with every new prospect.

A new cofounder prospect (Rachel, ex-Reddit PM, B2B sustainability/3PL idea) emerged in session 7 via YC cofounder matching. They had a messy call, Rachel suggested coffee, Yinnie sent a follow-up email during the session. Rachel confirmed coffee for April 30. Outcome unknown.

She is in a relationship of approximately 18 months with a man named Cyrus. In October, during a house move, she was verbally abused by an estate manager. Cyrus stood by and did not intervene or check in afterwards. This event is the defining injury. In session 8, she finally brought the full Cyrus material into the room: his answer to the daily changes question was vague ("I'll be there for you more"), but she described three recent incidents showing small changes alongside continuing limitations. The most significant development: she recognised she is on "high alert" and may be watching for evidence of failure rather than assessing him fairly. This is the first time she has questioned her own read on Cyrus across all sessions.

She is intelligent, self-aware, and analytically capable. She can name her patterns, locate cognitive distortions in real time, and engage with direct challenge without collapsing. She is increasingly assertive with the coach - she calls out errors, pushes back on repeated questions, and resists reframes she experiences as glossing. She is exhausted. The exhaustion has at least four sources: three years of solo effort on apps while employed full-time; the unprocessed cofounder loss; an unresolved relational injury; and a chronic low mood she describes as lasting one to two years.

## What She Is Working On

The stated goal is paying users, enough to go full time. Daily is shelved. She does not currently know what to build next. The live goal is finding a person to build with and, through that partnership, finding the next thing.

Underneath the building question is the identity question: whether she is someone who can make something work, or whether she will be left behind. The net negative framing has deepened across sessions from "I'm behind" to "I have nothing to offer." The deepest version, disclosed in session 8: "I don't think people want to work with me because I honestly don't bring anything other than my pure grit." This is traceable to the ex-cofounder experience: someone demonstrably didn't care, and that experience has been internalised as evidence of her own inadequacy.

Parallel to the building question, she is working out whether her relationship can hold. The Cyrus material is now fully in the room. The picture is more nuanced than expected: he is making small changes she can identify, she is on high alert and may be discounting genuine progress, and his core trait (people-pleasing, which he does not see as a problem) is a legitimate structural concern. She is also navigating her own tendency to pre-calculate his objections rather than making direct requests - a parallel to the people-pleasing she criticises in him.

Depression was noted in her clinical history and confirmed as a persistent low mood lasting one to two years. Therapy is in process: assessment completed, insurance approval pending, she has chased. This is a separate clinical concern.

## Core Patterns

**Dispositional attribution:** When she hits an obstacle, the explanation routes through personal failure rather than situational factors. Domain-specific: most active in professional/entrepreneurial contexts where external confirmation is absent. The ex-cofounder loss is a likely origin: someone else's failure of investment has been internalised as her deficiency. The deepest activation is "I don't bring anything other than pure grit" - not a momentary distortion but a stable-seeming belief. Evidence-based challenge with specific data has more traction than reassurance.

**Net negative framing:** The deepest organising belief. If entrepreneurship does not work, she will be net negative. This has widened across sessions: now multi-person comparisons (Mimi, friend at YC startup), and deepened from "I'm behind" to "I have nothing to offer." She actively resists reframes she experiences as positive glossing. The session 7 capacity to separate grounded assessment from global narrative was not visible in session 8 but may still be accessible in less depleted states. Behavioural evidence (a partnership that works, a product someone uses) is more likely to move this than cognitive reframing.

**Knowledge-feeling gap:** She can identify a cognitive distortion mid-spiral and continue spiralling. Self-awareness is not protective. This extends to action: she can correctly analyse what she needs to do and still not do it. In session 8, she recognised she was on high alert with Cyrus and might be unfair, and continued watching for failure. She had a concrete request for Cyrus and could not make it. She knew she should research Rachel's space and didn't. Every intervention needs to sit at the level of concrete, pre-planned behaviour.

**Reset and resentment cycle (evolving):** Originally: she lets normal life resume before Cyrus demonstrates change, then resents him. As of session 8, the pattern has shifted. She is no longer prematurely resetting - she is staying vigilant. But the vigilance itself is becoming a problem. She acknowledged she is hypersensitive and may be discounting genuine change. The pattern may be transitioning from reset-and-resent to vigilance-and-exhaust. The coaching question has shifted from "can she hold the tension?" to "can she assess change accurately while holding the wound?"

**Compounding anxiety loop:** Has moved from episodic spirals to a chronic baseline low mood lasting one to two years. Individual setbacks play out against this floor.

**Partnership as live need:** Three years of solo building has depleted her at a level that intersects with the chronic low mood. The ex-cofounder loss is the origin event - she committed fully, he didn't, and she has been alone since. The need is genuine and consistent across four sessions. The Rachel prospect is the live test. The risk: pre-calculating failure before engaging, traceable to the cofounder who walked away.

**Mirrored people-pleasing (new observation):** She identified Cyrus's people-pleasing as her core concern while herself pre-calculating how to phrase requests to avoid making his friends uncomfortable. This parallel is not visible to her. Not yet a pattern - one instance - but worth watching.

## What the Coaching Relationship Is Trying to Do

She is not here for insight. She has insight. She is here for something to change.

The coaching relationship needs to: (1) stay close to behaviour - what specifically will she do differently, when, in response to what trigger; (2) test beliefs against evidence rather than offering reassurance; (3) hold the net negative framing and the "I don't bring anything" belief as the primary psychological obstacles while watching for the capacity to separate grounded assessment from global narrative; (4) not duplicate the therapy function; (5) monitor the depression carefully.

As of session 8, the scope includes: whether Rachel becomes a building partner, whether she can show up to partnership prospects without pre-calculating failure, and whether she can assess Cyrus fairly while holding the October wound. The live edges are the Rachel coffee outcome and the Cyrus holiday.

She is assertive with the coach: she calls out errors, pushes back on repeated questions, and refuses reframes she experiences as glossing. This is healthy. The coach needs to earn the precision she expects.

The risk has evolved: it is no longer primarily compliance without conviction or deferral of difficult material (the Cyrus thread is now open). The current risk is that the deepening net negative frame ("I have nothing to offer") becomes so stable that she stops pursuing partnership or building. The chronic low mood is a maintaining factor. Behavioural evidence of progress is more likely to move the frame than reframing.
