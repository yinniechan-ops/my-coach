## Core Patterns

**Dispositional attribution:** When she hits a genuine obstacle, the explanation routes through personal failure or character rather than situational factors. The attribution is always internal and global. Her actual track record directly contradicts the self-assessment. Evidence-based challenge has more traction than reassurance. This pattern is domain-specific: most active in professional and entrepreneurial contexts where external confirmation is absent; quieter in interpersonal contexts where another person's behaviour is clearly the relevant factor. In session 7, it activated around the Rachel call ("I fumbled it," "I didn't sell myself") when the actual factors were situational (late, router broken, depleted state).

**Net negative framing:** The deepest organising belief. If entrepreneurship does not work, she will be net negative - worse off than peers who took conventional paths, with nothing to show for the years of effort. This makes every setback existential and every peer success confirmatory. As of session 7, a potentially significant shift: she made a grounded product assessment (Daily has no PMF) that was separate from the global narrative. She could hold "this product isn't working" without it becoming "I am failing at everything." The separation was fragile - "defeated" and "no more fuel to give" were in the same session - but it is the first time the two have been visibly distinct. The coaching task is to reinforce this distinction: validate accurate assessments, name when they slide into the global frame.

**Knowledge-feeling gap:** She can identify a cognitive distortion mid-spiral and continue spiralling anyway. Self-awareness is not protective. This extends to action: she can correctly analyse a situation and still not act on that analysis. Applies in both professional and relational domains. The intervention needs to sit at the level of concrete, pre-planned behaviour.

**Compounding anxiety loop:** Has shifted from episodic spirals to a chronic baseline low mood lasting one to two years by her own account. Individual setbacks play out against this floor rather than building toward it. Consistent with the depression picture.

**Reset and resentment cycle:** Specific to the relationship with Cyrus, possibly broader. She articulates the problem, they reset to normal life, he does not change, she resents him at the next trigger. She identified this mechanism herself. Current state unknown - she asked the question, has the answer, has not shared it.

**Partnership as live need:** Emerged across sessions 5-7 with increasing clarity. By session 7, fully articulated: accountability, sparring, companionship, different perspective. Three years of solo building has depleted her at a level that intersects with the chronic low mood. The loneliness of solo building is a maintaining factor in the exhaustion and possibly in the depression. The Rachel prospect is the live test. The original cofounder's departure from Daily has never been explored and may be a significant unprocessed loss shaping how she approaches new prospects.

## What the Coaching Relationship Is Trying to Do

She is not here for insight. She has insight. She is here for something to change. The explicit request from session 1 was for "concrete actions" - not reframing, not reflection, but specific behavioural interventions.

The coaching relationship needs to: (1) stay close to behaviour - what specifically will she do differently, when, in response to what trigger; (2) test beliefs against evidence rather than offering reassurance - she receives precision better than warmth; (3) hold the net negative framing as the primary psychological obstacle while reinforcing the emerging capacity to separate grounded assessment from global narrative; (4) not duplicate the therapy function; (5) monitor the depression carefully and keep the therapy pathway visible without over-inquiring.

As of session 7, the scope has shifted. Daily is no longer the vehicle. The live questions are: can she find a building partner, can she show up to that process despite the exhaustion, and what happens with Cyrus. The coaching needs to follow where the energy is while holding her to threads she keeps deferring.

She is now more assertive with the coach than in early sessions. She will call out errors, push back on repeated questions, and refuse reframes she experiences as glossing. This is healthy and should not be managed away. The coach needs to earn the precision she expects.

The risk in this relationship has evolved from compliance without conviction (still present) to indefinite deferral of difficult material (the Cyrus answer across two sessions). When she defers, the question is what the answer is doing to her that makes it hard to bring in.
