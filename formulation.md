# Formulation

**Last updated:** 2026-04-16 (session 2)

## Who Yinnie Is

Yinnie is a product manager, likely in her late twenties or early thirties, living in a country she relocated to - twice internationally by her own account. She is working a full-time job (current salary 85k, experienced as below market) that she experiences as daily confirmation of not having become what she wants to be. Alongside this, for nearly three years, she has been building apps independently - she has two working products, the most recent of which (Daily: an AI-powered personalised podcast aggregator that surfaces newsletters and notifications based on individual workflow) she is actively trying to get users for. A cofounder was involved at some point and left; this precipitated a return to employment and has not been explored in depth.

She is intelligent, self-aware, and analytically capable. She can name her patterns, locate cognitive distortions in real time, and engage with direct challenge without collapsing. She is also exhausted, and the exhaustion is not new.

## What She Is Working On

The stated goal is distribution: getting real users for Daily, building something sustainable outside of employment. Underneath this is a larger question about identity and worth - whether she is the kind of person who can make something work, or whether she will be left behind by peers who took conventional paths.

Depression was noted in her clinical history and is now confirmed in session as a persistent low mood and flatness that has been building for a while and is present beyond work frustration specifically. Therapy is in process: she has completed an assessment and is waiting for insurance approval. This is a separate clinical concern from the coaching work and should be held alongside it, not merged with it.

## Core Patterns

**Dispositional attribution:** When she hits a genuine obstacle, the explanation routes through personal failure or character rather than situational factors. The attribution is always internal and global. Her actual track record directly contradicts the self-assessment. Evidence-based challenge has more traction than reassurance.

**Net negative framing:** The deepest organising belief identified so far. If entrepreneurship does not work, she will be net negative - worse off than peers who took conventional paths, with nothing to show for the years of effort. This makes every setback existential and every peer success confirmatory. It is described as longstanding. It is not anchored to evidence: her actual history is one of initiative, movement, and output. Behavioural wins (a real user, a genuine piece of feedback) are more likely to shift it than reframing.

**Knowledge-feeling gap:** She can identify a cognitive distortion mid-spiral and continue spiralling anyway. Self-awareness is not protective. Interventions need to sit at the level of concrete, pre-planned behaviour - not reflection or insight.

**Compounding anxiety loop:** Anxiety from one episode carries forward and amplifies the next. This appears to have shifted from episodic (replaying specific meetings) to baseline: she describes a persistent low state rather than discrete spirals. Two and a half years of compounding may have raised the floor to a chronic flatness, consistent with the depression picture.

## What the Coaching Relationship Is Trying to Do

She is not here for insight. She has insight. She is here for something to change. The explicit request from session 1 was for "concrete actions" - not reframing, not reflection, but specific behavioural interventions that interrupt the patterns in real time.

The coaching relationship therefore needs to: (1) stay close to behaviour - what specifically will she do differently, when, in response to what trigger; (2) test beliefs against evidence rather than offering reassurance - she receives precision better than warmth; (3) hold the net negative framing as the primary psychological obstacle, not the app distribution problem, which is a vehicle not the destination; (4) not duplicate the therapy function - the emotional support lane exists, but the coaching lane should maintain a forward-facing, action-oriented structure; (5) monitor the depression carefully and keep the therapy pathway moving.

The risk in this relationship is compliance without conviction - she will agree to things she does not fully intend to do. Commitments need to be specific, small, and verified at the start of each subsequent session. If she has not done something, the question is not "why didn't you" but "what got in the way and what does that tell us."

## What Is Not Yet Clear

- Whether "I don't like being well known" is a genuine value or avoidance rationalisation (raised session 1, not yet resolved).
- What a good six-month outcome looks like for her in her own terms.
- The Holland and Barrett experience and what it did to her confidence.
- How the net negative framing interacts with her depression - which is primary and which is maintaining the other.
- What the second app is.
- Basic self-maintenance: sleep, rest, day-to-day functioning. May be relevant to the low mood picture.
